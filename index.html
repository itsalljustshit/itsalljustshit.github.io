fuck this.

fuck being told what to think. fuck influencers, politicians and billionaires, each equally convinced that they know how change will come. it still hasn't changed, it's still shit.

fuck why. we all have our own versions of why. let's stop arguing and decide what. and do it. let's start asking what can I actually do to make the world a better place?

work to improve your life.

work to minimise harm.

it sounds obvious. it is. it's obvious to everyone. life is shit, we all want it to be better, but we're so busy arguing over why it's shit that we forget we started all this because life is shit. so let's just do it. let's make it better for everyone by minimising the harm we do, and better for ourselves by working to improve our conditions. the more people that do this, the less shit it will be, so let's do it whenever we can, as much as we can.

if there's a group of you that can work to improve your lives then do it. form a group and work to do that. when you've solved that problem move on to the next one. you don't need a grand strategy to improve your life. you already know what's shit: work to fix it now! then focus on the next shit thing. form a new group for a new thing. if a group isn't useful then leave it. build your groups to improve your lives and minimise harm.

if you fuck up: work to minimise the harm as best you can. when your group fucks up: work to minimise the harm as best you all can. But if you worked to minimise harm when you did the fuck up, there will be far less harm to minimise after.

if a friend or another group asks for something, and you can do it whilst minimising harm, you should do it. this builds networks between people and groups, making everyone more connected. this gives you more people you can ask for help from. if you can't do it then say that and move on. if you think you need to stop something happening, you should stop it whilst minimising harm.

if you do this your life may get better or worse, but if you actively work to improve it whilst actively minimising harm it will be the best life it ever could have been.

it couldn't have been any better - but only if you always worked to improve it.

it couldn't have been any worse - but only if you always worked to minimise harm.

your life will be as good as your understanding of yourself and your conditions.

your life will be as bad as your understanding of harm.

practise learning about your conditions.

practise learning about harm done to you and others.

if you practise you'll get better at doing, but only learning how bad it is doesn't achieve anything. always ask:

is this harm actually needed? can I help minimise it?

is doing this going to make my life better or worse?

to put it simply:

don't just think it, do it!

your best life is on the line! do whatever you can, all the time! do it for every single decision you make!

if we all do this, it's a revolution.
